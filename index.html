Empirical Asset Pricing Analysis

A Comprehensive Study of Swedish Equity Markets

📊 6 Research Questions 📈 275 Months of Data 💼 71 Stocks + 10 Funds

Project Overview

This project provides a comprehensive empirical analysis of asset pricing models and portfolio theory using Swedish equity market data from 2003 to 2025. Through six interconnected research questions, we explore fundamental concepts in modern portfolio theory, including factor models, portfolio efficiency, market anomalies, and active management strategies.

Key Findings

💡

Factor Risk Premiums

Market risk premium of 8.41% annually, with significant size (SMB: 0.87%) and value (HML: 2.18%) premiums over the 23-year period.

🎯

Diversification Benefits

Investment funds cluster near the CML with significantly lower risk than individual stocks, demonstrating clear diversification benefits.

📊

Model Performance

Fama-French 3-Factor model provides better explanatory power than CAPM, especially for stocks with size or value tilts.

🔍

Size Effect

Post-2015 analysis reveals size-sorted portfolios exhibit significant alphas, suggesting CAPM doesn't fully capture the size premium.

💰

Fee Impact

While some funds show positive gross alphas (manager skill), fees often eliminate net alphas for investors—validating the efficient market hypothesis.

⚖️

Active Management

Treynor-Black framework demonstrates how to optimally combine mispriced securities with passive market exposure for Sharpe ratio maximization.
