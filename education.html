📖 Module 1: The Basics

What is Portfolio Theory?

Portfolio theory is the science of making smart investment decisions. It helps us answer fundamental questions like:

  • How much return should I expect from an investment?
  • How much risk am I taking?
  • How can I reduce risk without sacrificing returns?
  • Are my investments performing well compared to the market?

🎯 Key Concepts

Return 📈

How much money you make (or lose)

Formula: Return = (End Value - Start Value) / Start Value

Example: Buy stock at $100, sell at $110 → Return = 10%

In our data: Average market return = 8.41% per year

Risk 📉

How much returns fluctuate

Measured by: Standard deviation (volatility)

Example: If returns vary ±20%, that's your risk level

In our data: Market volatility = ~62% per year

Diversification 🎲

Don't put all eggs in one basket

Principle: Combining investments reduces risk

Why it works: When one goes down, another might go up

In our data: Funds have 40-60% lower risk than individual stocks

⚖️ Module 2: Risk & Return Tradeoff

The Fundamental Rule

Higher returns come with higher risk. There's no free lunch in finance!

This is why treasury bonds (safe) pay ~2%, while stocks (risky) average ~8-10%. The extra return is compensation for bearing risk.

🎮 Interactive: Risk-Return Calculator

Sharpe Ratio: Measuring Efficiency

The Sharpe ratio tells us how much return we get per unit of risk. It's like measuring "bang for your buck" in investing.

Sharpe Ratio = (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Risk

Higher is better! A Sharpe ratio above 0.5 is considered good.

✨ Module 3: The Magic of Diversification

Two Types of Risk

Systematic Risk 🌍

Also called: Market risk

What it is: Risk that affects the entire market (recession, interest rates, wars)

Can you avoid it? ❌ No - you get paid for this!

Unsystematic Risk 🏢

Also called: Idiosyncratic risk, firm-specific risk

What it is: Risk specific to one company (CEO quits, product fails)

Can you avoid it? ✅ Yes - through diversification!

The Capital Market Line (CML)

The CML shows the best possible risk-return combinations. Efficient portfolios lie on this line.

CML Formula: E(Rp) = Rf + Sharpe × σp

Where: E(Rp) = expected portfolio return, Rf = risk-free rate, σp = portfolio risk

💡 Insight: Individual stocks scatter below the CML (inefficient). Diversified funds cluster near the CML (efficient)!

📐 Module 4: The Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)

What is CAPM?

CAPM is the foundation of modern finance. It tells us how much return we should expect from any investment based on its systematic risk.

E(Ri) = Rf + βi × [E(Rm) - Rf]

E(Ri) = Expected return on asset i

Rf = Risk-free rate (1.61% in our data)

βi = Beta (measures systematic risk)

E(Rm) - Rf = Market risk premium (8.41% in our data)

Understanding Beta (β)

β = 1.0

Moves exactly with the market. If market goes up 10%, stock goes up 10%.

β > 1.0

More volatile than market. If β=1.5 and market goes up 10%, stock goes up 15%.

β < 1.0

Less volatile than market. If β=0.5 and market goes up 10%, stock goes up 5%.

Alpha: Outperformance

Alpha (α) measures how much better (or worse) an investment performs compared to CAPM's prediction.

  • α > 0: Outperforming! Earning more than expected for the risk taken.
  • α = 0: Fair return. Earning exactly what CAPM predicts.
  • α < 0: Underperforming. Earning less than expected.

In efficient markets, alpha should be zero. Persistent positive alpha is rare and valuable!

🔬 Module 5: Fama-French Three-Factor Model

Beyond CAPM

Professors Eugene Fama and Kenneth French discovered CAPM isn't enough. Two additional factors explain stock returns:

E(Ri) = Rf + βi(Rm-Rf) + si(SMB) + hi(HML)

📊 Market Factor (Rm - Rf)

What: Overall market risk premium

In our data: 8.41% per year

Interpretation: Compensation for bearing market risk

📏 Size Factor (SMB)

What: Small Minus Big

In our data: 0.87% per year

Interpretation: Small stocks outperform large stocks

📚 Value Factor (HML)

What: High Minus Low (book-to-market)

In our data: 2.18% per year

Interpretation: Value stocks outperform growth stocks

🎯 Module 6: Active Management & Treynor-Black

The Active vs Passive Debate

Passive Investing 📊

Strategy: Buy and hold market index

Pros: Low cost, simple, hard to beat

Cons: Can't outperform market

Fees: 0.2-0.5% per year

Active Investing 🎯

Strategy: Pick stocks to beat market

Pros: Potential for outperformance

Cons: High cost, hard to succeed

Fees: 1.0-2.5% per year

📊 The Fee Paradox: Real Data

💡 Key Finding: Some managers have positive gross alpha (skill exists!), but fees often eliminate net alpha for investors. Cost matters more than most think!

Treynor-Black: The Best of Both Worlds

Instead of choosing all-or-nothing, Treynor-Black shows how to optimally mix active and passive investing.

Portfolio = (Small Active Tilt) + (Large Passive Base)

Typical allocation: 20-30% active, 70-80% market index

Practical Takeaways

  • ✅ Cost matters most: A 1% fee costs ~25% of returns over 30 years!
  • ✅ Diversify systematically: Reduce idiosyncratic risk for free
  • ✅ Use factors wisely: Small-cap and value tilts have paid off historically
  • ✅ Be skeptical of alpha claims: True skill is rare and hard to identify
  • ✅ Start passive, tilt active carefully: Most wealth stays in market index
